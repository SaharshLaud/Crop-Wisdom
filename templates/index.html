About Us

We are the next generation of agricultural insights.

Agriculture is a crucial sector of the Indian economy, employing over half of the country's workforce and accounting for around 16% of its GDP.

Despite being the world's second-largest producer of food crops, Indian agriculture faces challenges like soil degradation, water scarcity, and climate change.

Our Crop and Fertilizer Recommendation System uses AI and machine learning to suggest the ideal fertilizer type and amount for each crop, reducing waste and increasing yields. Our goal is to make it accessible to all Indian farmers and empower them for a sustainable agricultural sector.

We analyze data on factors such as soil type, nutrient content, pH levels, crop type, planting date, and rainfall patterns to determine the optimal fertilizer use for Indian crops. Our system aims to reduce pollution and improve yields while making agriculture more sustainable and profitable for all farmers.

Crop Recommendation System

By performing the primary role of an agro consultant, this system provides farmers with personalized recommendations to optimize crop yield and increase profitability.

With the use of advanced algorithms, the crop recommendation system can accurately factor in relevant data to provide real-time crop recommendations that enable farmers to make informed decisions.

Overall, this technology has the potential to revolutionize the agricultural industry by providing farmers with valuable insights and improving crop production.

Fertilizer Suggestion System

The process of determining the right fertilizers for your crops can be simplified with our fertilizer suggestion tool.

All you need to do is input information about your soil's nutrient content and the crop you intend to grow. The tool then analyzes the data to identify any nutrient deficiencies or excesses in the soil and recommends appropriate fertilizers to address these issues.

With this approach, you can avoid wasting money on unnecessary fertilizers while ensuring that your crops receive the nutrients they need to thrive.
